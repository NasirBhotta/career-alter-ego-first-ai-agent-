There needs to be strict laws to regulate Large Language Models (LLMs) because, without robust legal frameworks, these powerful technologies pose significant risks to society that cannot be managed by voluntary guidelines alone. LLMs have the capacity to generate highly convincing misinformation, perpetuate biases, and infringe on privacy, all of which can cause widespread harm if left unchecked. Strict laws would enforce accountability on developers and users, ensuring transparency in data usage, ethical deployment, and mechanisms to prevent malicious exploitation. Furthermore, regulation is essential to safeguard against manipulative applications such as deepfake text, automated disinformation campaigns, and unauthorized surveillance, which threaten democratic processes and individual rights. Only through clear, enforceable legislation can we balance innovation with protection, ensuring that LLMs serve society’s interests without compromising safety, fairness, and trust.
